Core subjects are the easy part of a timetable. Everyone takes maths, everyone takes English, and the blocks more or less fall into place. Electives are where the trouble starts — because suddenly students in the same class are headed in different directions.

Get electives right and the rest of the timetable follows. Get them wrong and you'll spend the term untangling clashes.

## Why electives break timetables

The challenge with electives is parallelism. When a group of students splits across Art, Music, and Computer Science in the same block, all three classes must run **at the same time**, in **different rooms**, with **different teachers** — and no student can be in two of them.

Model that loosely and you get impossible schedules: a student assigned to two simultaneous electives, or a teacher expected to be in two rooms at once.

## OR groups vs. AND groups

schedU models elective choices with two simple building blocks.

### OR groups

An **OR group** is a set of mutually exclusive options — pick exactly one. A student choosing between Art, Music, and Drama belongs to one OR group; the scheduler guarantees the options run in parallel so the choice is always available and never conflicts.

### AND groups

An **AND group** is a set of subjects taken *together* — a required combination. A Science stream student who must take Physics, Chemistry, and Biology belongs to an AND group; the scheduler keeps all three on the timetable without overlapping them.

## Putting it together

Real schools mix both. A typical senior student might:

- Take a required **AND group** of three core sciences, and
- Choose one elective from an **OR group** of humanities options.

By declaring these relationships explicitly, you let the scheduler reason about them directly — instead of hoping a hand-built grid happens to work out.

## A quick checklist

Before you generate, confirm:

1. Every elective belongs to exactly one OR or AND group.
2. Parallel sections have enough rooms and teachers to run simultaneously.
3. Mutually exclusive options share the same time block.

Handle those three and your electives will slot in cleanly alongside the core timetable.
